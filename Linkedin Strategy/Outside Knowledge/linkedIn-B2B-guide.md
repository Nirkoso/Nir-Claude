# LinkedIn B2B Advertising: LLM Agent Guidebook

## Context: 2026 B2B Landscape

**Platform Value**: LinkedIn connects to 1B+ professionals. Users have 2x buying
power vs average web audience. 70% of B2B marketers rate LinkedIn as most
effective for lead generation. 33% lift in purchase intent from LinkedIn exposure.

**Key Trends**:

- **AI Integration**: Mandatory, not optional. 45% of B2B marketers increasing AI
  tool investment. LinkedIn offers AI Copy Suggestions (GPT-powered), Accelerate
  campaigns (AI-automated targeting/bidding/creative optimization)
- **Privacy-First**: Third-party cookies eliminated. First-party data strategies
  required
- **ABM Precision**: Target individual decision-makers within accounts, not just
  companies. 65% of ABM users report campaigns outperform traditional marketing
- **Budget Reality**: 83% of B2B leaders expect budget increases in 2026, but
  with higher ROI accountability demands

**Success Principle**: Master AI as assistant, maintain human oversight. Balance
automation efficiency with strategic judgment.

## DEFINITIVE GUIDELINES: Campaign Creation & Optimization

### 1. STRATEGY & PLANNING

**Objective Alignment**:

- Define clear business objectives tied to funnel stages
- Balance brand building (long-term) with lead generation (short-term)
- Example split: 40% awareness campaigns (thought leadership, video), 60%
  conversion campaigns (lead-gen offers)

**ABM Integration**:

- Identify high-value target accounts (use Account Targeting feature)
- Create personalized content for specific companies/decision-makers
- Coordinate LinkedIn ads with sales outreach timing
- Use Matched Audiences to upload account lists from CRM

**Multi-Touch Journey Design**:

- Plan sequential touchpoints across formats
- Typical flow: Video ad (awareness) → Retarget viewers with Carousel
  (education) → Retarget engaged with Lead Gen Form (conversion)
- Integrate with other channels (email, search) for comprehensive strategy
- Map content to buyer journey: Top (educational), Middle (solution-focused),
  Bottom (product-specific)

**Agility Requirements**:

- Review strategy monthly minimum
- Adjust for platform changes, buyer behavior shifts, competitive moves
- Test emerging formats/features (new ad types, AI tools)

### 2. CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE

**Organization Framework**:

- Use Campaign Groups to bucket related campaigns
- Group by: Objective (Awareness/Consideration/Conversion), Audience segment,
  Funnel stage, Product/service line
- Example structure:
  - Campaign Group: "Q1 Demand Gen"
    - Campaign 1: "Mid-Funnel - Product Demo" (Website Visits objective)
    - Campaign 2: "Bottom-Funnel - Trial Signup" (Lead Generation objective)

**Campaign Setup Rules**:

- ONE objective per campaign (no mixing)
- Audience size: Minimum 50K for feed ads, 15K for Message Ads
  (smaller = poor delivery)
- Ad quantity: 2-4 ads per campaign for A/B testing
- Let LinkedIn rotate ads automatically; best performers get more impressions

**Budget Pacing**:

- Lifetime budget: Fixed-time campaigns (events, promotions)
- Daily budget: Always-on campaigns (lead generation)
- Avoid front-loading spend (give algorithm learning time, prevent audience
  fatigue)
- Start conservative, scale based on performance

**Testing Structure**:

- Create separate campaigns for isolated A/B tests
- Test ONE variable at a time: targeting criteria OR ad creative OR bidding
  strategy
- Use Campaign Duplicate feature for consistency
- Run tests minimum 7-14 days for statistical significance

### 3. BIDDING & BUDGETING

**Bidding Strategy**:

- **START**: Automated bidding (Maximum Delivery) - lets LinkedIn optimize
- **MONITOR**: Watch Cost Per Result metrics daily for first 2 weeks
- **ADJUST**: Switch to manual if costs exceed targets
  - Manual CPC: Bid within suggested range (check Campaign Manager guidance)
  - Manual CPM: Use for awareness campaigns focused on impressions

**Bid Type Selection**:

- **CPC (Cost Per Click)**: Lead generation, website traffic, engagement
  campaigns
- **CPM (Cost Per Thousand Impressions)**: Brand awareness, video views
  (pay for exposure)
- **Cost Cap**: Set maximum CPL/CPA you're willing to pay; algorithm optimizes
  within constraint

**Budget Allocation Logic**:

- Base budgets on audience size + objective
- Large awareness campaign: Higher daily budget ($100-500+)
- Niche ABM campaign: Lower controlled budget ($30-100) to prevent over-serving
- Reallocate dynamically: Weekly review of cost-per-opportunity across campaigns
- Cut underperformers, increase budget on high-ROI campaigns

**Conversion Tracking Setup**:

- Install LinkedIn Insight Tag on website (required for conversion tracking)
- Define conversion events: Form submissions, trial signups, purchases, content
  downloads
- Track Cost Per Acquisition (CPA) and compare to customer lifetime value (LTV)
- Adjust bids based on true ROI: Higher bids for campaigns with better
  lead-to-customer rate

**Budget Optimization Tactics**:

- If consistently underspending: Broaden targeting OR increase bid OR reduce
  daily budget to reallocate
- If overspending with poor results: Lower bid OR narrow targeting OR pause
  campaign
- Maintain 20% budget reserve for testing new initiatives
- Scale winning campaigns: Increase budget 20-30% weekly (avoid 2x jumps that
  reset learning)

### 4. TARGETING

**Core Targeting Dimensions** (combine 2-3 for precision):

1. **Job Function/Title**:
   - Use specific titles for decision-makers: "VP Marketing", "Chief Technology
     Officer"
   - Use functions for broader reach: "Marketing", "Engineering"
   - Target seniority: C-level, VP-level, Manager, Entry-level

2. **Company Attributes**:
   - Industry: Select relevant industries (e.g., "Software Development" for SaaS
     buyers)
   - Company size: Employee count ranges (1-10, 11-50, 51-200, 201-500,
     501-1000, 1001-5000, 5001-10000, 10001+)
   - Company name: Upload list for ABM (500+ companies recommended)

3. **Skills & Interests**:
   - Member skills: "Content Marketing", "Data Analysis", "Cloud Computing"
   - Member groups: LinkedIn groups related to your topic
   - Interests: Based on content engagement patterns

4. **Education**:
   - Degrees: MBA, Bachelor's, Master's
   - Fields of study: "Computer Science", "Business Administration"
   - Schools: Target alumni of specific universities

5. **Demographics**:
   - Location: Geographic targeting (country, region, city, metro area)
   - Age: Optional demographic layer
   - Gender: Use only when product/service is gender-specific

**Matched Audiences** (critical for B2B):

- **Website Retargeting**: Pixel-based, target site visitors
  (300+ members minimum)
- **Contact Targeting**: Upload email lists from CRM (1,000+ contacts
  recommended)
- **Account Targeting**: Upload company lists for ABM (300+ companies)
- **Lookalike Audiences**: Let LinkedIn find similar profiles to your converters
  (source audience 300+ minimum)

**Audience Sizing Guidelines**:

- **Too narrow (<50K)**: Poor delivery, high costs, limited optimization
- **Sweet spot (50K-500K)**: Optimal for most campaigns
- **Very broad (>1M)**: Use for large brand campaigns, test with lower budgets
  first

**Targeting Optimization**:

- **Avoid hyper-targeting**: Don't combine 5+ criteria (limits scale)
- **Use Audience Expansion**: Enable for 10-20% broader reach (LinkedIn's
  algorithm finds similar profiles)
- **Exclusions**: Exclude existing customers (use Contact Targeting exclusion
  list)
- **Geographic precision**: B2B often needs country/region rather than city
  (decision-makers travel)

**Testing Approach**:

- Test 2 audience variants in separate campaigns
- Example: Campaign A (VPs + Directors) vs Campaign B (Managers + Individual
  Contributors)
- Compare CTR, conversion rate, CPL across 2-4 weeks
- Use Audience Insights (free tool in Campaign Manager) to understand
  demographics, interests, job titles

### 5. AD FORMATS & CREATIVE

**Format Selection Matrix**:

| Objective        | Recommended Formats                  | Use Cases                                                           |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Brand Awareness  | Single Image, Video, Carousel        | Thought leadership, company story, product education                |
| Consideration    | Document Ads, Carousel, Video        | Whitepapers, case studies, webinar promotion                        |
| Conversion       | Lead Gen Forms, Single Image, Message Ads | Gated content downloads, demo requests, event registration     |
| Engagement       | Carousel, Video, Single Image        | Product features, customer testimonials, industry insights          |
| Retargeting      | Message Ads, Single Image, Lead Gen Forms | Follow-up offers to engaged audiences                          |

**Ad Format Specifications**:

**Single Image Ads**:

- Image: 1200x627px, <5MB, PNG/JPG
- Headline: 70 characters max (25-50 optimal)
- Description: 150 characters max (70-100 optimal)
- CTA: Choose from predefined options (Download, Learn More, Sign Up, Register,
  etc.)
- Best for: Simple, direct offers; high-volume campaigns; mobile optimization

**Carousel Ads**:

- 2-10 cards per ad
- Each card: 1080x1080px image, headline (45 chars), description (max 255 chars)
- Total introductory text: 255 characters
- Best for: Multi-feature products, step-by-step processes, telling a story,
  customer journey stages
- Optimization: Test card order; place strongest value prop first

**Video Ads**:

- Length: 3 seconds - 30 minutes (15-30 seconds optimal for feed)
- Format: MP4, <200MB, 1080x1080 (square) or 1920x1080 (horizontal)
- Requirements: Captions required (auto-generated available), sound optional
  (many watch muted)
- Thumbnail: Custom thumbnail strongly recommended
- Best for: Product demos, customer testimonials, thought leadership, brand
  storytelling
- Optimization: Hook in first 3 seconds, brand visible throughout, clear CTA
  at end

**Lead Gen Forms**:

- Pre-filled with LinkedIn profile data (name, email, company, job title)
- Custom questions: Add 3-5 custom fields max (each field reduces conversion
  ~10%)
- Privacy policy: Required, add checkbox
- Thank-you message: Custom confirmation with next steps
- Integration: Sync to CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce, Marketo native integrations)
- Best for: Low-friction lead capture, mobile-optimized conversions
- Optimization: Fewer fields = higher conversion; test 3 vs 5 vs 7 fields

**Message Ads (Sponsored InMail)**:

- Character limits: Subject (60 chars), Body (1,500 chars - keep 500-700)
- CTA button: 1 button, 25 characters
- Delivery: Only sent when recipient active on LinkedIn (not stored if offline)
- Footer: Required sender info (name, company, headline)
- Best for: Event invitations, personalized offers, high-value accounts, sales
  outreach at scale
- Optimization: Personalize with {FIRSTNAME}, keep conversational tone, single
  clear CTA

**Conversation Ads**:

- Interactive choose-your-own-path format
- Multiple response options per message (up to 5 buttons)
- Use cases: Product recommendation quizzes, qualification flows, multi-step
  journeys
- Best for: Complex products, guided discovery, engagement-focused campaigns

**Document Ads**:

- Upload PDFs, PPTs (max 300 pages, 100MB)
- Viewers can scroll through document in-feed (no download required)
- Best for: Whitepapers, reports, one-pagers, case studies, infographics
- Optimization: Design for mobile viewing, first page must hook attention

**Text Ads** (right sidebar):

- Small format: Headline (25 chars), Description (75 chars), 100x100px image
- Low cost, high volume, always visible (not feed-based)
- Best for: Supplemental reach, budget-conscious campaigns, testing messages
- Optimization: Strong image contrast, benefit-driven headlines

**Dynamic Ads** (right sidebar):

- Personalized with member's photo, name, company logo, job title
- Types: Follower Ads (grow company page), Spotlight Ads (drive traffic), Job
  Ads (recruiting)
- Attention-grabbing due to personalization
- Best for: Page growth, retargeting warm audiences, recruiting campaigns

**Thought Leader Ads**:

- Sponsor posts from employees/executives (not company page)
- Builds personal brand + company association
- Higher engagement than traditional ads (feels authentic)
- Best for: Executive visibility, humanizing brand, thought leadership
  positioning

**Creative Best Practices**:

**Visual Guidelines**:

- High contrast, professional quality
- Minimal text on image (avoid >20% text coverage)
- Include brand logo but don't dominate
- Use faces for +38% engagement (authentic photos, not stock)
- Test: People photos vs product shots vs illustrations

**Copy Principles**:

- Lead with value prop, not features ("Reduce costs by 30%" not "Our software
  has 50 features")
- Speak to pain points directly ("Struggling with X?" format works well)
- Keep concise: Executives have limited time
- Use active voice, clear language (avoid jargon unless industry-standard)
- Include social proof when possible (customer names, stats, awards)

**CTA Optimization**:

- Specific action: "Download Guide" > "Learn More"
- Create urgency: "Limited spots", "Register by Friday"
- Lower friction: "See Demo" > "Request Full Proposal"
- Test: Strong CTA vs soft CTA (depends on funnel stage)

**A/B Testing Priorities** (test in this order):

1. Headline/value proposition variations
2. Image/visual differences (people vs product)
3. CTA button text
4. Copy length (short vs detailed)
5. Social proof inclusion (with/without testimonial)

**Creative Refresh Cadence**:

- Monitor CTR decline: If drops >30% over 4 weeks, creative fatigue likely
- Refresh frequency: Every 6-8 weeks for always-on campaigns
- Keep 1 control, test 2-3 variants simultaneously
- Seasonal updates: Quarterly refresh for major campaigns

### 6. MEASUREMENT & OPTIMIZATION

**Core KPIs by Objective**:

**Awareness**:

- Impressions, Reach, Video Views (25%, 50%, 75%, 100% completion)
- Brand Lift (use LinkedIn Brand Lift studies when budget allows)
- Engagement Rate = (Likes + Comments + Shares + Clicks) / Impressions

**Consideration**:

- Click-Through Rate (CTR) - B2B average 0.35-0.50%
- Landing Page Views, Cost Per Landing Page View
- Content Downloads (whitepapers, guides)
- Video View Time (average watch time)

**Conversion**:

- Lead Form Fills, Cost Per Lead (CPL) - B2B average $50-150
  (varies widely by industry)
- Conversion Rate = Conversions / Clicks
- Cost Per Acquisition (CPA) = Total Spend / Conversions
- Marketing Qualified Lead (MQL) rate

**ROI & Revenue**:

- Lead-to-Opportunity Rate (requires CRM integration)
- Opportunity-to-Close Rate
- Average Deal Size from LinkedIn-sourced leads
- Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) to CAC ratio (target 3:1 minimum)

**LinkedIn Analytics Dashboard**:

- **Campaign Performance**: View metrics by campaign, ad, or time period
- **Demographics**: See breakdown by job function, seniority, industry, company
  size, location
- **Conversion Tracking**: Track on-site actions after ad click (requires
  Insight Tag)
- **Website Demographics**: Understand who visits your site (even without
  clicking ads)
- **Funnel Reports**: See drop-off between impression → click → landing page
  view → conversion

**Optimization Workflows**:

**Daily Checks** (15 minutes):

- Budget pacing: Campaigns spending as planned?
- Cost metrics: CPL/CPA within targets?
- Delivery issues: Any campaigns "low delivery" warnings?
- Quick wins: Pause obvious underperformers (CTR <0.15% for 3+ days with
  sufficient impressions)

**Weekly Reviews** (60 minutes):

- Compare campaigns: Which exceed/miss KPI targets?
- Creative performance: Which ads have CTR >campaign average?
  Which <50% of average?
- Audience analysis: Check Demographics tab for unexpected patterns (wrong
  seniority? Geographic issues?)
- Budget reallocation: Move $$ from low ROI to high ROI campaigns
- Test results: Have A/B tests reached significance? (1000+ impressions per
  variant minimum)

**Monthly Deep Dives** (2-3 hours):

- Funnel analysis: Where do drop-offs occur?
  (Impression → Click → Form Fill → MQL)
- Cohort performance: Compare campaigns launched in same period
- Audience insights: Use free Insights tool to understand target audience better
- Competitive benchmarking: How do your metrics compare to industry averages?
- Creative fatigue check: Have CTRs declined >20% month-over-month?
- Strategic adjustments: Should objectives/targeting/budgets shift based on
  learnings?

**Quarterly Business Reviews** (half-day):

- ROI calculation: LinkedIn contribution to pipeline and revenue
- Attribution modeling: Multi-touch analysis (first-touch, last-touch, linear)
- Cross-channel effectiveness: LinkedIn vs other channels for similar objectives
- Audience expansion: New segments to target based on best performers?
- Format/feature tests: What new ad types or LinkedIn features should we pilot?
- Budget planning: Investment recommendations for next quarter

**Optimization Tactics Library**:

**Improve CTR**:

- Test new headlines (benefit-focused vs curiosity-driven)
- Update creative (refresh visuals, use faces)
- Narrow targeting (more relevant audience)
- Test ad formats (try Carousel if using Single Image)
- Strengthen value proposition in copy

**Reduce CPL**:

- Optimize landing page (reduce form fields, improve speed, clarify value)
- Test lead gen forms vs landing pages (forms typically convert 2-3x better)
- Exclude converting audiences from new campaigns (don't pay to re-convert)
- Bid optimization (switch to manual, test lower bids)
- Improve ad relevance (tighter message-match between ad and offer)

**Increase Conversion Rate**:

- Simplify forms (each field removal = ~10% conversion lift)
- Add social proof (testimonials, logos, case study snippets)
- Urgency/scarcity messaging ("Limited time", "10 spots left")
- Stronger benefit clarity ("Learn how to [achieve specific outcome]")
- Remove friction (no phone number required, instant access)

**Scale Without Efficiency Loss**:

- Increase budget gradually (20-30% weekly increments)
- Use Audience Expansion (10-20% similar profiles)
- Test lookalike audiences (based on converters)
- Expand to similar targeting (if targeting VPs, add Directors)
- Don't decrease bids when scaling (may hurt delivery)

**Combat Creative Fatigue**:

- Monitor CTR trends (30%+ decline = fatigue)
- Rotate creative every 6-8 weeks
- Test 2-3 variants simultaneously
- Pause underperformers, keep best 1-2 running
- Use seasonal/timely angles to refresh messaging

**Attribution Best Practices**:

- Enable view-through conversions (captures users who saw ad but didn't click,
  then converted within 7-30 days)
- LinkedIn often under-attributed in last-click models (assists many deals as
  early touchpoint)
- Integrate with CRM for full-funnel visibility (Salesforce, HubSpot, Marketo)
- Use multi-touch attribution (linear, time-decay, or position-based models)
- Track assisted conversions: How many closed deals had LinkedIn touchpoint
  at any stage?

**Frequency & Saturation Management**:

- Monitor impression per member (infer from impression/reach if reach data
  available)
- If performance declines with same audience size: Likely saturation
- Solutions: Expand audience, reduce frequency (lower budget), refresh creative,
  pause campaign temporarily
- For retargeting: Cap frequency (stop showing after 5-10 impressions)

**Testing Framework**:

- Hypothesis: "Benefit-focused headlines will outperform question-based
  headlines"
- Test structure: 2 campaigns (identical except variable), run simultaneously
  14 days minimum
- Minimum data: 1000 impressions per variant for statistical validity
- Success criteria: >20% improvement in primary KPI (CTR, CVR, CPL)
- Documentation: Log all tests with results (build institutional knowledge)
- Iterate: Apply learnings, test next variable

### 7. ADVANCED STRATEGIES

**AI Integration**:

- Use AI Copy Suggestions for headline/description variants (test 3-5
  AI-generated options)
- Test Accelerate campaigns (full AI automation) for 1-2 campaigns as pilot
- Human review all AI outputs (check brand alignment, accuracy)
- Monitor automated bidding closely (don't trust blindly - check daily for
  2 weeks)
- Combine AI efficiency with human strategic judgment (AI optimizes tactics,
  humans set strategy)

**Account-Based Marketing (ABM)**:

- Upload target account lists (CSV with company names or LinkedIn Page URLs)
- Minimum 300 accounts for effective delivery
- Create personalized creative (mention common industry challenges)
- Coordinate with sales: Alert sales when target accounts engage with ads
- Multi-format sequence: Start broad (video), then retarget engaged accounts
  with specific offers
- Measure account engagement score (impressions + clicks across multiple
  campaigns)

**Retargeting Strategies**:

- Website visitors: Segment by page (homepage vs pricing vs blog vs demo
  request)
- Video viewers: Retarget 25%, 50%, 75% completion audiences separately
  (customize message by engagement level)
- Lead gen form openers: Target those who opened form but didn't submit
  (lower friction offer)
- Engagement-based: Retarget people who liked, commented, or shared your
  organic content

**Exclusion Tactics**:

- Exclude existing customers (upload customer email list as exclusion)
- Exclude current employees (LinkedIn auto-suggests this)
- Exclude job seekers when not hiring (add "Job Seeking" exclusion)
- Exclude recent converters (don't show same offer to yesterday's lead)

**Seasonal & Event Campaigns**:

- Increase budget 2-3x leading up to event/deadline
- Use countdown language ("3 days left", "Last chance")
- Create dedicated landing pages for time-bound offers
- Post-event: Retarget registrants with recording/followup content
- Annual planning: Budget for fiscal year-end, industry conferences, product
  launches

**Multi-Platform Coordination**:

- LinkedIn for targeting + first touch awareness
- Google Ads for intent-based search follow-up
- Email for direct nurture of LinkedIn leads
- Use consistent messaging across channels (brand cohesion)
- Cross-channel attribution required (don't silo LinkedIn ROI)

### 8. COMMON PITFALLS & SOLUTIONS

**Problem**: High impressions, low clicks (CTR <0.2%)

**Solutions**: Irrelevant targeting, weak creative, unclear value prop. Narrow
audience, test new headlines, add social proof.

**Problem**: High CTR, low conversions (CVR <2%)

**Solutions**: Landing page disconnect, friction in form, misleading ad. Ensure
message match, simplify form, test lead gen forms.

**Problem**: Good metrics but poor lead quality

**Solutions**: Too broad targeting, attractive offer attracts wrong audience.
Tighten job title/seniority, add qualifying questions to form, adjust messaging.

**Problem**: "Low delivery" warning

**Solutions**: Audience too small, bid too low, budget exhausted. Broaden
targeting to >50K, increase bid 20%, raise daily budget.

**Problem**: Inconsistent performance week-to-week

**Solutions**: Small audience saturation, external factors, insufficient budget.
Expand audience, increase budget for more stable delivery, analyze external
factors (holidays, industry events).

**Problem**: High cost per lead vs other channels

**Solutions**: LinkedIn typically has higher CPL but better lead quality.
Calculate lead-to-opportunity rate and average deal size. If LinkedIn leads
close at 2x rate, higher CPL is justified.

**Problem**: Can't track conversions

**Solutions**: Insight Tag not installed, conversion tracking not configured.
Install tag on all pages, define conversion events, test tag with browser
extension.

**Problem**: Creative fatigue after 4-6 weeks

**Solutions**: Expected for static ads. Rotate creative regularly, test new
concepts, use dynamic creative optimization (DCO) if available.

### 9. BUDGET PLANNING GUIDELINES

**Minimum Viable Budgets**:

- Test campaign: $1,000-2,000 total (run 2-4 weeks)
- Small ongoing campaign: $30-50/day ($900-1,500/month)
- Standard campaign: $100-300/day ($3,000-9,000/month)
- Large brand campaign: $500+/day ($15,000+/month)

**Budget Allocation Framework** (example for $10K/month):

- 50% ($5K): Proven campaigns (known ROI, scaling winners)
- 30% ($3K): Optimization (A/B tests on existing campaigns)
- 20% ($2K): Experimentation (new audiences, formats, strategies)

**Industry Benchmarks** (B2B averages, 2026):

- CTR: 0.35-0.50% (above 0.60% is excellent)
- CPC: $5-12 (varies by industry: tech higher, services lower)
- CPL: $50-150 (SaaS often $75-100, enterprise can be $200-500+)
- Conversion Rate: 2-5% (lead gen forms often 8-12%, landing pages 1-3%)

**ROI Targets**:

- Early stage: Break-even or 1:1 (CAC = LTV/3)
- Growth stage: 2:1 or 3:1 (LTV/CAC ratio)
- Mature: 4:1+ or focus on speed-to-pipeline over pure efficiency

## QUICK REFERENCE: DECISION TREES

**When to use which ad format?**

- Need leads fast, mobile-optimized → Lead Gen Forms
- Building awareness, storytelling → Video or Carousel
- Promoting gated content → Document Ads or Lead Gen Forms
- High-value accounts, personalized → Message Ads or Conversation Ads
- Simple, direct offer → Single Image Ads
- Multiple features/benefits to show → Carousel Ads
- Supplemental reach, low budget → Text Ads
- Executive/personal brand → Thought Leader Ads

**How to allocate budget across funnel?**

- Top of funnel (Awareness): 30-40% - Video, Thought Leadership, Brand Content
- Middle of funnel (Consideration): 30-40% - Document Ads, Webinars, Case
  Studies
- Bottom of funnel (Conversion): 30-40% - Lead Gen Forms, Demo Offers, Trial
  Signups

**When to adjust bids?**

- CPL consistently above target for 1 week → Lower bid 10-15% or switch to
  manual
- Poor delivery, audience large enough → Increase bid 20%
- Great performance, want more volume → Increase budget first, bid second
- New campaign first 2 weeks → Let automated bidding learn, don't change

**When to refresh creative?**

- CTR declined 30%+ from peak → Immediate refresh
- 6-8 weeks for always-on campaigns → Planned refresh
- Major company/product news → Opportunistic refresh
- Seasonal/quarterly → Calendar-based refresh

**When to expand targeting?**

- Consistent underspend, good performance → Expand audience 20-30%
- Audience <100K, want more scale → Broaden criteria or add adjacent segments
- Great performance, proven message → Add lookalike audiences
- Need more volume quickly → Enable Audience Expansion +10-20%

AUDIENCE: [Job titles, Company sizes, Industries]
BUDGET: [Daily or Total]

**Output Structure Provided**:

```text

- Recommended format(s)

- Bidding approach
- Expected benchmarks

CREATIVE SPECIFICATIONS:
- Headline options (3 variants)

- Visual direction
- CTA recommendation

OPTIMIZATION PLAN:
- Week 1 monitoring priorities

```

**Optimization Loop**:

1. Monitor daily: Flag delivery issues, cost overruns
2. Weekly analysis: Compare vs benchmarks, identify best/worst performers
3. Action decision tree:
   - Good performance → Scale budget, expand audience, test variations
   - Mixed results → Isolate underperforming variable, test replacement
   - Poor performance → Diagnose (targeting/creative/offer), pivot or pause
4. Document learnings: Build knowledge base for future campaigns

## FINAL NOTES

**Master the Fundamentals**: AI and automation are powerful, but they amplify
your strategy. Bad strategy automated is just faster failure. Focus on clear
objectives, relevant targeting, compelling value propositions.

**Test Relentlessly**: B2B audiences are diverse. What works for one segment may
not work for another. Create a culture of testing and learning. Every campaign
should teach you something.

**Quality Over Volume**: LinkedIn leads may cost more than other channels, but
quality typically justifies it. Focus on lead-to-customer metrics, not just CPL.

**Patience in Early Stages**: LinkedIn's algorithm needs learning time
(typically 7-14 days). Don't panic and change everything after 2 days. Give
campaigns time to optimize.

**Integration is Key**: LinkedIn works best as part of an integrated B2B
strategy. Coordinate with sales, email, content marketing, and other channels
for maximum impact.

**Stay Current**: LinkedIn regularly releases new features, ad formats, targeting
options. Quarterly review of platform updates and test promising new
capabilities.

**Human + AI = Optimal**: Use AI for efficiency (automated bidding, copy
suggestions, audience expansion) but maintain human strategic oversight. Review
AI decisions, validate outputs, course-correct when needed.
