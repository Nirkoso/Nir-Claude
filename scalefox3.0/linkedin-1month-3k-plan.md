# LinkedIn Campaign Plan: 1 Month, $3K Budget

**For:** First-time B2B LinkedIn advertisers
**Goal:** Generate qualified leads at <$150 CPL
**Timeline:** 30 days
**Total Budget:** $3,000

---

## Executive Summary

This plan follows proven LinkedIn best practices for first-time advertisers:
- **Week 1:** Warm-up phase ($30/day) - Build algorithm trust
- **Weeks 2-4:** Scale strategically with 70/20/10 budget split
- **Expected:** 25-40 qualified leads, 5-10 demos booked, $80-150 CPL

**Keep it simple:** 1-2 campaigns, 2-4 ads, 15 min/day management.

## Campaign Structure

### Campaign 1: Warm-Up (Week 1)
**Budget:** $210 ($30/day for 7 days)
**Objective:** Website visits
**Goal:** Test creative, build algorithm trust, gather initial data

**Setup:**
- **Audience:** 100K-300K (Job titles: VP Marketing, Director Marketing, Head of Growth | Company: 10-500 employees | Industry: B2B SaaS, Software, Professional Services)
- **Creative:** 2 single image ads (test different hooks)
- **Bidding:** Maximum Delivery (automated)
- **Daily limit:** $35 (safety cap)

**Success Metrics:**
- ‚úì Spending full daily budget (no delivery issues)
- ‚úì CTR >0.25%
- ‚úì 50-70 clicks total
- ‚úì CPL <$500 (yes, it'll be high - that's normal for Week 1)

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't optimize yet - just collect data
- Don't panic at high CPL - Week 1 is expensive
- Don't change creative before Day 7

---

### Campaign 2: Core Offer - Lead Generation (Weeks 2-4)
**Budget:** $2,100 total ($100-115/day)
**Objective:** Lead generation
**Goal:** Drive qualified demos/trials at <$150 CPL

**Setup:**
- **Audience:** Same as Week 1, refined based on demographics that clicked
- **Creative:**
  - Keep winning ad from Week 1
  - Add 1-2 new variants (different value props)
  - Use Lead Gen Forms (pre-filled LinkedIn data = higher conversion)
- **Bidding:** Cost Cap bidding at $150 per lead

**Ad Creative Strategy:**

**Ad A: Problem Hook**
- **Headline:** "Proving marketing ROI shouldn't take spreadsheet gymnastics"
- **Image:** Dashboard screenshot showing clear attribution
- **CTA:** "See how it works"

**Ad B: Social Proof**
- **Headline:** "Why 200+ B2B marketers switched to [Your Product]"
- **Image:** Customer logos or testimonial quote
- **CTA:** "Get a demo"

**Success Metrics:**
- ‚úì CTR >0.35%
- ‚úì CPL $100-150
- ‚úì Lead form completion rate >50%
- ‚úì 20-30 leads total

---

### Campaign 3: Brand Awareness (Weeks 2-4)
**Budget:** $600 total (~$20/day)
**Objective:** Engagement (likes, shares, comments)
**Goal:** Build warm audience pool for retargeting

**Setup:**
- **Audience:** Same core audience
- **Creative:** Thought leadership carousel or short video (30-45 sec)
- **Format:** Carousel (5 slides): "5 Mistakes B2B Marketers Make with Attribution"

**Success Metrics:**
- ‚úì Engagement rate >2%
- ‚úì Video view rate >50% to 75%
- ‚úì 300+ engaged users (your retargeting pool)

---

### Campaign 4: Retargeting (Weeks 3-4)
**Budget:** $300 total (~$20/day)
**Objective:** Conversions
**Goal:** Convert warm leads at <$100 CPL

**Setup:**
- **Audience:**
  - Website visitors (last 30 days)
  - Video viewers (25%+ completion)
  - Lead form openers who didn't submit
- **Creative:** Urgency-driven or case study ad
- **Example:** "You checked us out last week. See why [Customer] made the switch."

**Success Metrics:**
- ‚úì CTR >0.50% (warm audience should perform better)
- ‚úì CPL $60-100
- ‚úì 5-10 leads

## Optimization Rules (Decision Triggers)

### When to PAUSE an ad:
- CTR <0.20% for 3+ consecutive days
- CPL >$250 for 5+ consecutive days
- Frequency >4.0 (ad fatigue)
- Lead form completion rate <40%
- 5+ unqualified leads in a row

### When to SCALE an ad:
- CTR >0.50% for 3+ consecutive days
- CPL <$100 for 5+ consecutive days
- Lead quality is high (>60% qualified)
- Frequency <2.5 (still fresh)
- Budget utilization >90%

**Scaling Rule:** Increase budget by 30-50% max per adjustment. Wait 3-5 days before next change.

### When to REFRESH creative:
- CTR drops 30%+ from previous week
- Frequency >3.5
- Week 6+ running same ad

## Emergency Playbooks

### Scenario 1: $500 spent, 0 leads
**Likely Cause:** Targeting too narrow or creative isn't resonating

**Fix:**
1. Check audience size (expand if <50K)
2. Test new ad with stronger value prop
3. Simplify lead form (reduce fields to 3-4)
4. Pause campaign for 24h, relaunch with changes

---

### Scenario 2: Lots of leads, all unqualified
**Likely Cause:** Targeting too broad or offer attracts wrong audience

**Fix:**
1. Narrow targeting (add seniority filter, specific company sizes)
2. Update ad copy to call out ICP specifically
3. Add qualifying question to lead form
4. Adjust offer (make it more specific to your ICP)

---

### Scenario 3: CTR dropping week over week
**Likely Cause:** Ad fatigue

**Fix:**
1. Check frequency (if >3.5, pause ad for refresh)
2. Create 2-3 new ad variants with different hooks
3. Test new imagery (faces, product screenshots, data viz)
4. Expand audience to reduce frequency

---

### Scenario 4: CPL increasing week over week
**Likely Cause:** Audience saturation or declining creative performance

**Fix:**
1. Refresh creative (new hooks, new images)
2. Expand audience (lookalike or new job titles)
3. Increase retargeting budget (cheaper CPL)
4. Test new campaign objective (switch from clicks to conversions)

## Month 2 Planning Guide

### If Month 1 was SUCCESSFUL (CPL <$150, 5+ qualified demos):
**Strategy:** Scale winners, expand audience

- Increase budget to $5K-7K
- Launch lookalike audiences based on converters
- Test new creative angles (2-3 new variants)
- Add video content
- Expand to new geographies or industries

---

### If Month 1 was ACCEPTABLE (CPL $150-200, 3-5 demos):
**Strategy:** Optimize before scaling

- Keep budget at $3K
- Focus on creative refresh (5-10 new ads)
- Test Lead Gen Forms vs. landing pages
- Narrow targeting to best-performing segments
- Double down on retargeting (20% ‚Üí 30% of budget)

---

### If Month 1 was UNDERPERFORMING (CPL >$200, <3 demos):
**Strategy:** Pause and diagnose

- Reduce budget to $1.5K
- Deep audit: Is the offer right? Is the ICP right?
- Test dramatically different creative approaches
- Consider switching from LinkedIn ads to organic content
- Evaluate if LinkedIn is the right channel for this ICP
